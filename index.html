Ir. Soekarno

The Proclamator of Indonesian Independence

Here's a timeline of Ir. Soekarno's life:

  • 1901 - Born in Surabaya, Dutch East Indies
  • 1921 - Begins studies at Technische Hoogeschool (now Bandung Institute of Technology)
  • 1926 - Graduates with a degree in Civil Engineering
  • 1927 - Founds the Indonesian National Party (PNI) to promote independence from Dutch rule
  • 1929 - Imprisoned by Dutch colonial authorities for political activities
  • 1942 - Japanese forces occupy Indonesia during World War II; Soekarno collaborates with them to gain support for independence
  • 1945 - Proclaims Indonesia's independence on August 17, 1945
  • 1949 - Officially recognized as President of Indonesia after the Dutch transfer sovereignty
  • 1955 - Hosts the Bandung Conference, promoting solidarity among Asian and African nations
  • 1965 - Survives an attempted coup, leading to a shift in power dynamics in Indonesia
  • 1967 - Forced to relinquish the presidency to Suharto
  • 1970 - Dies under house arrest in Jakarta

"To build a free nation, there are thousands of obstacles to face, but let us move forward, and fight, so that this nation remains victorious and sovereign."

-- Ir. Soekarno
